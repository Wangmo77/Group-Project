BLOOD TYPES

Every blood type is needed to help patients who count on lifesaving transfusions. But what you may not know is that each blood type can help in different ways. Learn more about how your blood type can help patients!

Blood Types

A Positive (A+)

A+ is the second most common blood type. Can donate to A+ and AB+. Can receive from A+, A-, O+, and O-.

A Negative (A-)

A- is a rare blood type. Can donate to A+, A-, AB+, and AB-. Can only receive from A- and O-.

B Positive (B+)

B+ is fairly common. Can donate to B+ and AB+. Can receive from B+, B-, O+, and O-.

B Negative (B-)

B- is a rare blood type. Can donate to B+, B-, AB+, and AB-. Can only receive from B- and O-.

AB Positive (AB+)

AB+ is the universal recipient. Can receive from all blood types but can only donate to other AB+.

AB Negative (AB-)

AB- is very rare. Can receive from AB-, A-, B-, and O-. Can donate to AB+ and AB-.

O Positive (O+)

O+ is the most common blood type. Can donate to all positive blood types. Can receive from O+ and O-.

O Negative (O-)

O- is the universal donor. Can donate to all blood types but can only receive from O-.
