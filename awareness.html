Blood Donation Awareness

Your single donation can save up to three lives. Join us in our mission to create a sustainable blood supply for Samtse and beyond. Learn how you can make a difference today.

Why Donate Blood?

Saves Lives

Every blood donation can help up to three different patients in need. Your contribution could be the difference between life and death for someone in your community.

Community Support

Blood donation strengthens our community by ensuring that blood is available for emergencies, surgeries, and patients with chronic conditions.

Health Benefits

Regular blood donation can help reduce iron overload, lower risk of heart disease, and stimulate production of new blood cells.

The Donation Process

1

Registration

Complete a simple registration form and provide basic information about your health history.

2

Health Screening

A quick health check including blood pressure, hemoglobin level, and temperature measurement.

3

Donation

The actual donation takes about 10-15 minutes. You'll be comfortable throughout the process.

4

Refreshment

After donation, you'll rest for a short while and have some refreshments to help your body recover.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does donating blood hurt?

You may feel a slight pinch when the needle is inserted, but the donation process itself is generally painless. Most donors compare it to a mild pinprick sensation.

How long does the entire process take?

The entire process, from registration to post-donation refreshments, typically takes about 45-60 minutes. The actual blood donation only takes 10-15 minutes.

How often can I donate blood?

Men can donate blood every 3 months, while women can donate every 4 months. This allows your body enough time to replenish the donated blood cells.

Is it safe to donate blood?

Yes, donating blood is very safe. All equipment used is sterile, disposable, and used only once. Our staff are trained professionals who follow strict safety protocols.

What should I do before donating?

Eat a healthy meal before your donation, drink plenty of fluids, and get a good night's sleep. Avoid fatty foods and alcohol 24 hours before donation.
